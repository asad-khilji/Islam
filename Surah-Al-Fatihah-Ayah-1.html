Allah says I have divided the prayer between Me and My slave into two halves

Half of it is for Me and the other half is for My slave

When we say Praise and thanks is for Allah, Master of all nations and people

Allah says My slave has praised Me

When we say the extremely loving and caring right now, the always extremely loving and caring

Allah says My slave has exalted Me

When we say Master of the day of judgment

Allah says My slave has glorified Me

When we say You alone we worship and You alone we ask for help

Allah says This is between Me and My slave and My slave shall have whatever he or she is asking for

When we say Guide us to the straight path

Allah says It is guranteed

When we say the path of those whom You have favored

Allah says It is guranteed

When we say But not the path of those who are the receipents of rage

Allah says It is guranteed

When we say nor of the lost

Praise and Thanks is for Allah

Praise and Thanks are two different ideas

You can praise without thanking

You can thank without praising

You can praise the ocean but you do not thank the ocean

Musa thanks the Pharoah for raising him, but does not praise him as a god

Ibrahim thanks his father for raising him, but does not praise his worship of the idols

Allah completes the phrase and says praise and thanks is for Allah

We are praising and thanking Allah at the same time

We must have two attitudes in mind when saying Alhamdulillah, praise and thanks

This statement makes us optimistic

We must have a genuine reason to praise Allah and thank Him at the same time

We cannot praise Allah and complain at the same time

This gives us a positive attitude in life

Our deen begins with optimism and a positive attitude

This is a complete change in thought and attitude

Genuine and Sincere Praise and Thanks is for Allah

Gratitude is limited to a reaction

Allah uses the word Hamd and not shukr

Hamd is a gratitude that is not limited to a reaction

Allah does not need us to praise and thank Him

There is a difference between nouns and verbs

Nouns are timeless and not stuck in a time past, present, or future

A noun does not need anyone to do it and it is independent

Verbs are stuck in time being either in past, present, or future tense

A verb requires someone to do it and it is dependent on someone

Praise and Thanks is a noun

The praise and thanks for Allah is timeless and independent

Allah does not need anyone to praise and thank Him

Whether we praise and thank Allah or not the praise and thank to Allah still exists

If Allah commanded us to praise Him

Then praise and thanks will exist only when we do it

So Allah did not issue us a command to praise and thank Him

There are two kinds of sentences

Information sentences and emotional sentences

By saying Praise and Thanks is for Allah it is a statement of information and emotion

By saying For sure praise and thanks is for Allah it is a statement of information only

Allah says praise and thanks is for Allah so it is both informational and emotional sentence

Surah Al Fatihah says Praise and Thanks is for Allah

Surah Al Jathiya says Praise and Thanks is only for Allah

Praise and Thanks is only for Allah is only used when debating with someone

Surah Al Fatihah is a statement from the heart and not debating with anyone

Surah Al Jathiya is debating with someone

Allah introduces Himself to us in Surah Al Fatihah

So Allah uses His proper name which is Allah

By using the name Allah you call upon all the names and attributes of Allah

Allah uses His proper name Allah and it clears any confusion of praise and thanks being for anyone else

Some praise and thanks could be for other people

The Ultimate, Genuine, Sincere, and Timeless Praise and Thanks is for Allah who is independent from his creation

Rabb consists of five names and attributes

The first name is Malik or Master and Owner

The second name is Sayyed or the one who is in contol and in charge

The third name is Murrabi or the one who makes sure something grows, develops, improves, and takes care of it constantly

The fourth name is Qayyim or someone one who makes sure something gets better and better constantly

The fifth name is Munnim or the giver of gifts

Everything Allah has given us is a gift

We are not entitled to anything because it is gifted to us by Allah

We cannot complain about anything because we do not own anything

Allah is our Master and we are His slaves

Slavery has some negative connotations, but Allah is a unique Master who gives gifts

Human beings have this delusion of freedom

We are enslaved to someone and something like money, fashion, people, government

We want to be free

You hate when people tell you what to do

When you enslave yourself to Allah then you are free from everything else

We are always slaves of Allah 24/7

Salah is a practical demonastration that we are slaves of Allah

So after every salah we become a better slave of Allah
